# Acme Books — Finance & Bookkeeping Policy Handbook

## Invoicing and payment terms
Standard payment terms are Net 30 from the invoice date. Approved long-standing clients may be granted
Net 45 with manager sign-off. Every invoice must show an invoice number, issue date, due date, itemised
services, currency, and accepted payment methods. Preferred payment method is bank transfer or Wise.

## Accounts receivable and collections
An invoice is considered overdue the day after its due date. The collections schedule is: a friendly
reminder at 3 days overdue, a firmer follow-up at 14 days, and a final notice at 30 days overdue.
Overdue accounts are grouped into aging buckets of current, 30, 60, and 90-plus days. Collection emails
are always reviewed by a human before sending.

## Accounts payable and vendor bills
Vendor bills require a matching purchase reference before approval. Before any payment run, bills are
screened for duplicates by comparing vendor, amount, and date. Suspected duplicate bills are held and
flagged for review rather than paid. Payment runs are scheduled weekly on Wednesdays.

## Expense reimbursement
Employees submit expense claims with an itemised receipt within 30 days of the expense. Claims without a
valid receipt are not reimbursed. Approved reimbursements are paid in the next scheduled payment run.
Meals and travel have per-diem caps set annually by the finance manager.

## Refunds and credit notes
Refunds are issued only against a paid invoice and require finance manager approval. A credit note is
raised to document the refund and is applied to the customer's account. Refunds are processed within 10
business days of approval.

## Month-end close
The month-end close is completed within five business days of month end. Steps include reconciling bank
and payment accounts, matching payments to invoices, reviewing the AR aging report, and posting accruals.
Bank and FX accounts are reconciled manually.

## Data handling and security
Client financial data is accepted as CSV or Excel exports only. No access to a client's live accounting
system is required or requested. De-identified sample data is used for any demonstration. Files are
deleted after an engagement ends unless retention is agreed in writing.
